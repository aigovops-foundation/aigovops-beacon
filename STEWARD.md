# Founding Steward Partner

## Glacis Technologies, Inc

The AiGovOps Foundation names [**Glacis Technologies, Inc**](https://www.glacis.io/) as its **Founding Steward Partner**.

Glacis is the steward of the [OVERT 1.0 open standard](https://overt.is/), an inaugural sponsor of the AiGovOps Foundation, and the technical voice we want shaping the bar for every open-source project that carries the Foundation's name.

## What "Steward Partner" means here

This is a working role, not an honorific. It is also a role we are explicitly **asking Glacis to define** — because the steward should define their own engagement model, not have one imposed by the project they're stewarding.

This document is a **v0.1 placeholder**. The intent is for Glacis to revise, replace, or supersede it. The Foundation will publish whatever the Steward writes as the canonical version.

## v0.1 placeholder — what we are proposing as a starting point

We propose the Steward Partner is responsible for:

1. **Technical bar.** Defining the conformance bar for every Foundation-affiliated open-source project, anchored on the standard the project implements (OVERT for Beacon; future standards as adopted).
2. **Pre-release review.** Chairing the [Technical Review Committee](GOVERNANCE.md) that reviews every Foundation-affiliated implementation before public release.
3. **Profile registration recommendations.** Recommending profile registration to the standard registry on behalf of Foundation projects.
4. **Crosswalk authority.** Approving crosswalk mappings between Foundation materials and the underlying standard, so the community speaks one vocabulary.
5. **Engagement protocols.** Defining (see [`ENGAGEMENT.md`](ENGAGEMENT.md)) how member companies engage with the Foundation in a way that is helpful to commercial steward(s) and to the community simultaneously — the FinOps model, adapted.
6. **Member company onboarding.** Setting the rules of the road for how new companies join the program without diluting either the steward's commercial position or the community's trust.

In return, the Foundation commits to:

1. **Co-marketing.** Public attribution as Founding Steward Partner on the Foundation site, every Foundation project README, and at every Foundation event.
2. **First profile.** Beacon is registered as the first OVERT Protocol Profile under the Foundation's program.
3. **Pre-release coordination.** No Foundation project ships publicly without Steward review. This is a rule, not a courtesy.
4. **Charter veto.** The Steward Partner can object to any Governing Board decision that materially affects the technical track. Objections are public, written, and trigger a 30-day pause.
5. **Tenure stability.** The Steward Partner is named for an indefinite term, reviewed every 24 months.

## What we want Glacis to write

We want Joe and Glacis to write, on their letterhead and in their voice, the canonical version of:

- **This document** (`STEWARD.md`) — the steward's role, the steward's commitments, what good stewardship looks like, what removes a steward.
- **`ENGAGEMENT.md`** — how a company engages with the Foundation's program: contribution, sponsorship, profile registration, co-marketing, conflict resolution. The FinOps/CNCF playbook, adapted to AI governance.
- The criteria for **graduating from a profile-in-progress to a fully registered OVERT Protocol Profile**.
- The criteria the TRC uses for **pre-release review**.

We will accept the Steward's text as the canonical text, and merge it as written, subject only to Foundation board sign-off on items that have direct business-track impact (budget, brand, legal).

## Why we are asking Glacis to define this — not defining it ourselves

Three reasons.

**First, you know how this is supposed to work, and we are learning.** We are first-time community-builders. You have shipped OVERT, raised real capital, navigated AI2, and you understand the FinOps / Linux Foundation / CNCF playbook in your bones. We do not. The community is better off if the protocol comes from you.

**Second, the steward's authority is only credible if the steward defines it.** A foundation that imposes a role on its steward has a steward in name only. We want the real thing.

**Third, this is the structural answer to "how does this not happen again."** If the Steward Partner sets the engagement protocol, defines the pre-release review rules, and chairs the TRC, then "Foundation ships adjacent thing without attribution" is a procedural impossibility, not a promise. That is a stronger guarantee than any apology.

## Acceptance

This document becomes canonical when:

1. Glacis (via Joe Braidwood or designated successor) signs off in writing.
2. The AiGovOps Foundation Governing Board ratifies the signed-off version.
3. The signed-off version is committed to this repo and announced jointly.

Until then: **v0.1, proposed, awaiting Steward revision.**

## Removal and succession

The Steward Partner may resign at any time with 60 days' notice. The Governing Board may remove the Steward Partner only by a 2/3 vote with cause. Succession is by Governing Board appointment from within the standards community, prioritizing continuity with the standard then in use.
