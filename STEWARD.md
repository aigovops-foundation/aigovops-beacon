# Project Steward

## Glacis Technologies, Inc — founding

The AIGovOps Foundation invites [**Glacis Technologies, Inc**](https://www.glacis.io/) to be the founding Project Steward of this repo, and of the wider AIGovOps Foundation open-source program.

Glacis is the steward of the [OVERT 1.0 open standard](https://overt.is/) — the standard Beacon implements. Glacis sponsored the Foundation's inaugural event. They've been generous with their time and ideas. And the most honest answer to *"how do we make sure the project stays close to the standard"* is *"have the people who steward the standard steward the project too."*

This is an invitation, not a position. We've written down what we think the role looks like; the steward is welcome to rewrite all of it.

## What we are asking the steward to do

In plain language:

1. **Set the bar.** Decide, in the open, what "good enough to ship" looks like for any Foundation-affiliated project that touches the standard. We follow it.
2. **Look at things before they ship.** Chair a small review circle that reads every public release before it goes out. We built Beacon once without that habit; we don't want to do it again.
3. **Speak for the standard inside the project.** If something in the project drifts from the standard, the steward says so and we fix it.
4. **Help us figure out the engagement model.** How does another company get involved without diluting your commercial position or the community's trust? We've started sketching that in [`ENGAGEMENT.md`](ENGAGEMENT.md). We want your version.
5. **Be the public co-voice on launches.** When the profile registers, when a new framework crosswalk lands, when the next project joins the program — you and the Foundation say it together.

## What we commit to in return

1. **Attribution where it belongs.** Glacis and OVERT on the homepage, the README, the receipt schema, and at every event we run. Already done as of commit [`1913613`](https://github.com/bobrapp/aigovops-beacon/commit/1913613).
2. **No surprises.** No Foundation-affiliated project ships publicly without the steward and the review circle seeing it first. This is a habit, kept by the people involved, not a clause anyone enforces.
3. **The first registered profile.** Beacon is the first OVERT Protocol Profile under the Foundation's program. We aim to make it a good example.
4. **The right voice in the room.** If the steward thinks the Foundation is about to do something that hurts the project, we pause and listen. There is no formal veto because there is no formal contest — we are building this together or we are not building it at all.
5. **A seat that lasts as long as it serves.** No fixed term, no expiration, no review schedule that turns this into a transaction. The arrangement lasts as long as it is good for the community and the standard.

## What we want Glacis to write

On your letterhead, in your voice, replacing what is here:

- The canonical version of **this document** — what good stewardship looks like, what removes a steward, what good faith looks like on a hard day.
- The canonical version of **[`ENGAGEMENT.md`](ENGAGEMENT.md)** — how a company engages with the Foundation in a way that is helpful to commercial stewards and to the community at the same time. The FinOps / CNCF / Linux Foundation playbook, adapted to AI governance.
- The criteria the review circle uses for pre-release review.
- The criteria a profile graduates by — from in-progress, to AAL-1 self-declared, to AAL-2 with an Independent Attestation Provider, and onward.

When you write it, we publish it as written. We may have edits — we'll send them as PRs against your text, not as conditions on it.

## Why we are handing you the pen

Three honest reasons.

**One:** you know how this is supposed to work, and we are learning. You've shipped OVERT, navigated AI2, raised real capital, and you understand the FinOps and Linux Foundation playbooks from the inside. We don't. The community is better off if the pattern comes from you.

**Two:** a steward whose authority is set by the project they steward is a steward in name only. If you set the bar, you actually set the bar. That is the version we want.

**Three:** it is the real answer to *"how does this not happen again."* If you write the engagement protocol, chair the review circle, and define what graduates a profile, then "Foundation ships an adjacent thing without attribution" is no longer something we can do by accident. That is a stronger guarantee than any apology, and a more durable one.

## If it stops working

Either side can walk away.

If the steward decides this is no longer a good use of their time, we thank them in public, write down what we learned in an ADR (see [`docs/decisions/`](docs/decisions/)), and find another steward who is close to whatever standard the project then implements.

If the project decides the stewardship is no longer serving the community, we say so in the open and have the same conversation.

We hope neither of those happens. We are writing this down because saying it kindly, in advance, is better than hoping we never have to.
