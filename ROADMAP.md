# Roadmap

This is what we're trying to get done. It's a living doc. If you want something on it, send a PR.

## Next 30 days (by mid-June 2026)

- **Profile registration submitted** to the OVERT Protocol Profile registry (`aigovops-beacon.v1`, AAL-1 self-declared). See [`docs/PROFILE_REGISTRATION.md`](docs/PROFILE_REGISTRATION.md).
- **Steward review of v0.2 governance docs** — Joe and the Glacis team rewrite [`STEWARD.md`](STEWARD.md), [`ENGAGEMENT.md`](ENGAGEMENT.md), and any other governance doc they want, on their voice.
- **First public community call.** Public link, agenda in [`MEETINGS.md`](MEETINGS.md), notes in the repo.
- **First review circle** stood up — steward + two maintainers + one community seat. Public agenda. Public notes.
- **Joint launch post** with Glacis announcing the profile registration and the steward arrangement.
- **First ADRs** under [`docs/decisions/`](docs/decisions/) — OVERT alignment and steward-led governance.

## Next 90 days (by mid-August 2026)

- **AAL-1 self-declared conformance review** chaired by the steward. Findings published in the repo.
- **Close the gaps in [`crosswalks/overt-mapping.yaml`](crosswalks/overt-mapping.yaml)**:
  - Behavioral drift detection (OVERT §Agentic AI Controls).
  - Delegation-chain attestation.
  - Auditor-reproducible confidence intervals in scoring.
- **Second member organization** joins the program — ideally an implementer not a vendor.
- **Sector pack: healthcare.** A practical bundle of checklists, crosswalks, and walkthroughs for clinical AI deployments, advisory not normative.
- **The fact-check appendix becomes a habit.** Every Foundation project ships with a `*-claim-review.md` doc. We move from one-off audit to standing practice.

## Next 6 months (by mid-November 2026)

- **AAL-2 with an Independent Attestation Provider.** Identify the IAP, scope the engagement, complete the assessment, publish.
- **Second open-source project** in the Foundation program, also OVERT-aligned, also reviewed by the same review circle before any public launch. We'd like the steward to pick the topic.
- **First joint Foundation × Steward field report** — what real implementers learned standing this up. Co-authored, co-published.
- **Engagement model finalized** — Joe's version of [`ENGAGEMENT.md`](ENGAGEMENT.md) replaces the v0.2 placeholder. Membership tiers, contributor norms, and pre-disclosure habits all settled.
- **Foundation community call cadence** — proven, public, sustainable.

## A year out (by May 2027)

- **At least three registered OVERT Protocol Profiles** under the Foundation program. We are no longer the only one.
- **Public conformance reports** for each registered profile, published quarterly.
- **A second steward** in conversation, if the standards landscape calls for it. Not before.
- **A clean answer to "how did you avoid the captured-foundation trap?"** — written down, lived out, demonstrable.

## What is not on this roadmap (on purpose)

- **A standards-issuing program.** We implement OVERT. We do not issue standards. See [`STANDARDS.md`](STANDARDS.md).
- **A commercial product.** Beacon is Apache 2.0. Companies are welcome to build commercial offerings on or around it; the Foundation doesn't.
- **A certification program.** Conformance lives in OVERT's process, not ours.
- **Anything that competes with the steward's commercial roadmap.** That is the whole point of having a steward.

## How to add to this roadmap

Open a PR. Make the case in the description. The steward and the maintainers will look at it together.
