# Meetings

We meet in the open. Here is where, when, and how to join.

## Community call — monthly

**When:** first Wednesday of the month, 10:00 AM PT (US/Pacific).
**Where:** Google Meet link posted to the GitHub Discussions tab the day before, and to [aigovopsfoundation.org](https://www.aigovopsfoundation.org/) the week before.
**Who:** anyone.
**Format:** 30 min agenda, 15 min open Q&A, optional 15 min show-and-tell at the end.
**Notes:** captured in [`docs/meetings/`](docs/meetings/) within 48 hours.
**First one:** to be scheduled with the steward — see [`ROADMAP.md`](ROADMAP.md).

## Review circle — bi-weekly

**When:** every other Tuesday, 9:00 AM PT.
**Where:** Google Meet. Link in the agenda issue.
**Who:** the project steward (chair), two maintainers, one rotating community seat. Anyone can read; only members of the circle have agenda items.
**Format:** 45 minutes. What's about to ship, what needs review, what's blocked.
**Notes:** committed to [`docs/meetings/review-circle/`](docs/meetings/review-circle/) within 48 hours.
**Agenda:** opened as a GitHub issue tagged `review-circle` the Friday before, so anyone can add items.

## Steward office hours — monthly (when the steward has time)

**When:** the steward picks a window each month and posts it. Always public, always optional.
**Where:** wherever the steward prefers.
**Who:** anyone in the community who wants to ask the steward something directly.
**Format:** drop-in. No agenda required.

## Quarterly retrospective

**When:** once a quarter, joined to the broader [AIGovOps Foundation](https://www.aigovopsfoundation.org/) community.
**Where:** see Foundation site.
**Who:** anyone.
**Format:** "shipping safe / staying safe / getting back to safe" — what landed, what we learned, what to change.

## Annual standard-and-implementation summit

**When:** once a year, in person if we can swing it.
**Where:** to be decided with the steward.
**Who:** steward, member companies, maintainers, practitioners, regulators if they'll come.
**Format:** state of the standard, state of the implementations, what the standard needs next.

## How meetings are different from issues and PRs

If a decision can be made in writing, it lives in an issue, a PR, or an ADR ([`docs/decisions/`](docs/decisions/)) — not in a meeting. Meetings are for things that need a conversation: roadmap shifts, hard PR reviews, unclear questions to the steward, community kindness.

If a meeting produces a decision, the decision goes into the repo within 48 hours. If it isn't in the repo, it didn't happen.

## How to add a meeting

Open a PR. Tell us why. We'd rather have fewer recurring meetings and more good asynchronous habits.
